Purpose: This demo page shows how the aviation dashboard displays different flight restriction levels based on weather conditions.

The system uses a concentric restriction model where more restrictive conditions automatically apply to all less experienced pilot categories.

Flight Category Legend

Category A: No solo restrictions in effect
Category B: Student pilot solos restricted (surface winds >15 kt, crosswind >10 kt, ceiling <1,500 ft AGL, or visibility <3 SM)
Category C: Private pilot solos restricted (surface winds >25 kt or crosswind >15 kt)
Category D: Instrument/Commercial solos restricted (surface winds >30 kt or crosswind >20 kt)
Category F: No flights — airport closed per announcement

Note: Categories B/C/D are also triggered by ceiling <1,500 ft AGL, visibility <3 SM, or temperature/heat index thresholds. The highest applicable category takes precedence (D > C > B > A).

These restrictions apply independently of wind/visibility restrictions above

Summer Day (High Heat Index)

Temperature: 95°F

Heat Index ≥ 95°F: Diamond Solo Restricted

Key Benefits of This System

  • Clear Decision Making: One status tells everyone immediately what operations are allowed
  • Safety-First Approach: More restrictive conditions automatically protect less experienced pilots
  • Reduced Confusion: No need to check multiple pilot category restrictions
  • Visual Clarity: Color-coded status cards provide instant recognition
  • Comprehensive Information: Displays exact restricting factors including wind speeds, crosswind components, heat index, and temperature restrictions with emoji indicators (🌬️ 🌡️ ❄️ ☁️ 👁️)
  • Real-time Updates: Status updates automatically every 5 minutes with fresh weather data
  • Temperature Safety: Automatically accounts for both extreme heat (≥105°F heat index) and extreme cold (≤5°F) restrictions
